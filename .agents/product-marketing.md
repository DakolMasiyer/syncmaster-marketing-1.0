# SyncMaster — Product Marketing Context

## Product

**SyncMaster** is an infrastructure platform that bridges African composers and global sync licensing. It solves a structural gap: African composers produce world-class music but lack the rights documentation, metadata standards, and industry access required to place tracks in film, TV, ads, and games. SyncMaster vets composers, prepares their catalogues for sync, and connects them to active production briefs.

**Core value proposition:**
- To composers: get your music into global productions — legally clean, professionally submitted, brief-matched — without a label or agent.
- To music supervisors: receive 5 curated, rights-cleared, brief-matched African tracks in 48 hours. No clearance admin.

**What makes it different:**
- Every composer is vetted: rights confirmed, metadata cleaned, stems prepared
- Only one-stop cleared tracks enter the catalogue (one conversation, one deal)
- Brief-response time: 24–48 hours from brief receipt to delivery
- Building in public: the whole process is documented and posted in real time

## Personas

### Tunde (Primary — Composer)
- Independent African composer (Lagos, Abuja, Nairobi, Cape Town, Accra)
- Has professional-quality work but has never earned from sync
- Understands streaming; doesn't understand sync infrastructure
- Pain: talent isn't the problem — rights complexity, metadata gaps, no access to briefs
- Goal: turn catalogue into a consistent secondary (or primary) income stream
- Language: practical, specific, numbers-driven. Responds to proof and education.

### Amara (Secondary — Music Supervisor / Production Company)
- Music supervisor at a streaming platform, ad agency, or film production
- Actively looking for African music but frustrated by clearance complexity
- Pain: finding African music that's rights-clean, brief-matched, and delivered on time
- Goal: reduce turnaround time from brief to approved track; access diverse, cleared African catalogue
- Language: efficiency, speed, rights certainty, professional delivery

### Denzel (Tertiary — Senior Global Supervisor)
- Senior Music Supervisor, Head of Music, or Creative Director (Music) at a major production house, streaming platform, sports brand, or global ad agency
- Clients/context: HBO, Netflix, A24, EA Sports, Pepsi, Champions League, Oscars-level film, global brand campaigns
- Budget authority: $250K+ music budgets; can approve multi-track and catalogue licensing deals
- Location: LA, London, New York, Amsterdam, Paris
- Pain: every major library sounds the same; authentic African music is hard to source at rights-clean quality and at their speed and scale; one bad rights situation = legal and PR exposure at this level
- Goal: find music that makes a cultural statement — sounds their audience doesn't know they're waiting for — before it becomes mainstream
- Language: efficiency, indemnification, catalogue depth, professional delivery at volume — and a genuine hunger to discover something the competition hasn't found yet

## Positioning

**Category:** Sync licensing infrastructure / African music placement agency

**Frame:** Not a music library. Not a label. An infrastructure company that makes African composers commercially viable in the global sync market.

**Against:** General music libraries (they don't focus on African music or vet for sync-readiness), labels (they don't service independent composers at this level), cold-pitch culture (SyncMaster replaces cold pitching with brief-matched structured responses)

**Differentiation:** Speed (24–48h turnaround), vetting quality (every composer is rights-confirmed before a brief arrives), focus (exclusively African composers, global ambition)
