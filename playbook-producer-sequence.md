# Producer Outreach Sequence (Amara)

## Overview
3-touch LinkedIn sequence targeting music supervisors and producers to educate, build trust, and drive demo requests. Aligned with Proof and Industry content pillars.

## Sequence Goals
- Educate on SyncMaster's solution to supervisor pain points (Industry pillar)
- Build trust through proof of placements and workflow efficiency (Proof pillar)
- Drive demo requests by demonstrating time savings and quality
- Align with existing content calendar themes

## LinkedIn Message 1: Education - "The Sync Problem You're Actually Trying to Solve"

**Send**: Day 0 (After connection request accepted)
**Trigger**: New connection (from LinkedIn search, event, referral, content engagement)
**Goal**: Educate, create awareness, position SyncMaster as the solution
**Content Pillar**: Industry (5% focus) + Education (35% focus)

### Message:
```
Hi [First Name],

Came across your work on [specific project/show/brand] — wanted to reach out.

Quick question: when you're briefing for African music, what's the biggest frustration you hit?

For most supervisors I talk to, it's not finding the music — it's the 40% of workflow time spent on rights administration instead of creative selection.

We built SyncMaster to flip that: less time on clearance, more time on creative selection.

Worth 15 minutes to see how it works in practice?
```

### Alignment:
- Personalized opener shows genuine interest
- References actual pain point from content: rights admin taking 40% of time
- Uses Industry pillar tone: Sharp · Authoritative · Analytical
- Low-pressure ask for 15-minute exploration
- Mirrors language from Industry pillar content

## LinkedIn Message 2: Proof - "What Supervisors Are Actually Seeing"

**Send**: Day 3 (if no reply to Message 1)
**Trigger**: No response to initial message
**Goal**: Build credibility, show social proof, demonstrate workflow
**Content Pillar**: Proof (25% focus)

### Message:
```
Hi [First Name],

A few numbers from this month that are relevant to what I mentioned:

Brief-to-delivery: 31 hours (down from 42)
Rights confirmation: 5 hours (down from 9)
Rights issues in placements: 0

One supervisor's note after their first brief: "We returned 5 curated options in 31 hours. The producer has never moved this fast."

The difference isn't the music — it's having the rights work done before the brief arrives.

If you're solving briefs where African music could fit, I can show you what we'd send back.
```

### Alignment:
- Pulls specific metrics from PLAN-kanban-5-fixes.md and content
- Uses Proof pillar tone: Aspirational · Credible
- Includes supervisor quote that mirrors actual content
- Focuses on workflow efficiency (key producer pain point)
- Soft CTA for demo

## LinkedIn Message 3: Application - "How We Match Your Brief in 24 Hours"

**Send**: Day 7 (if no reply to Message 2)  
**Trigger**: No response to second message
**Goal**: Drive action, demonstrate process, lower barrier to try
**Content Pillar**: Behind the Scenes (20% focus) + Proof

### Message:
```
Hi [First Name],

Curious how we actually work with supervisors like you? Here's our brief-to-placement window:

Monday 9am: Brief arrives ("45-sec open. Global NGO campaign. Hopeful, organic, African. One-stop. Delivery: 48 hours.")
Monday 9-11am: Catalog search, 5 tracks shortlisted per brief with stems
Monday 11am-2pm: Tracks delivered to supervisor with rights confirmation docs
Tuesday: Supervisor responds - one track shortlisted for picture review
Tuesday 2-5pm: Rights confirmation requested, 3-hour turnaround, documents sent
Wednesday: Placement confirmed. Licence terms in negotiation.

Total: 31 hours from brief to delivery.  
Rights work: 5 hours.  
Creative selection time: 26 hours.

We operate in 24 hours because every composer is vetted, every track tagged, every stem ready, every rights document filed.

If you have a brief coming up where African music could work, I'd love to run it through our system and show you what we'd send back.
```

### Alignment:
- Directly mirrors IG-M2-BTS-02 content (brief-response week breakdown)
- Uses Behind the Scenes pillar tone: Personal · Honest · Direct
- Shows concrete, believable workflow
- Focuses on time savings for creative work
- Specific, low-pressure CTA
