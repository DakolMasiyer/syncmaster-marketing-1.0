DAY 2 Email Metadata check — here's what to fix ▾
Subject Lines — A/B
Metadata check — here's what we found and what to fix
3 metadata issues that block placements (and how to fix them this week)
Body
Hey [First Name], We've looked at your tracks. Here's the honest assessment. Metadata is the part most composers skip. It's also what determines whether a supervisor ever finds your music — and whether royalties reach you when they do. The 4 fields that matter most for sync placements: 1/ TITLE & COMPOSER CREDITS Exactly as they appear on your publishing registration. Even one character difference creates a rights dispute. Check it against your PRO registration now. 2/ ISRC CODES Every commercial recording needs one. Your distributor issues them for free — DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby all do this automatically. If you're self-distributing, your national music body can assign them. 3/ BPM, KEY, MOOD TAGS Supervisors search by feel before they search by genre. "Hopeful, organic, 90 BPM" gets a brief match. "Afrobeats" alone does not. Add at least 3 mood tags and 2 instrumentation tags per track. 4/ DURATION Every track needs a full-length version and a 30-second edit. Most briefs specify duration. One version = half the briefs automatically. Reply with your current metadata spreadsheet (or we'll send you our template). We'll flag any gaps and help you fix them before the file submission step. The ISRC and credits check is the one thing that can delay your go-live — so let's confirm it this week.
