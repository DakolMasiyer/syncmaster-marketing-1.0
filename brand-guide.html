01 · Brand Strategy

Mission, Positioning & Model

The foundational layer. Everything — copy, design, product — should be derivable from these statements.

Mission
"Connect African composers to global screens.
Curated, cleared, and ready."
Category
Sync licensing infrastructure / African music placement agency
Frame
Not a music library. Not a label. An infrastructure company that makes African composers commercially viable in the global sync market.
Differentiation
Speed — 24–48h brief-to-delivery. Vetting — every composer rights-confirmed before a brief arrives. Focus — exclusively African composers, global ambition.
Model
15–20% commission on placement fees, charged to the producer. No placement = no fee.
Market
$500M+ global sync market annually. Africa-first — no direct competitor exists.
Vision
Phase 1: curated concierge. Phase 4: open-but-vetted marketplace when manual matching no longer scales.
SyncMaster IS
Infrastructure platform bridging African composers and global sync licensing
Rights-clearing, metadata-cleaning, submission-preparation service
A curation engine matching composer catalogue to active production briefs
One-stop clearance provider — single conversation, single deal
Building in public — full transparency on process, numbers, failures
A concierge in Phase 1; evolving to vetted marketplace at scale
SyncMaster IS NOT
A music library or discovery database
A record label
A cold-pitch or blind-submission platform
A streaming or playlist placement service
A general music industry tool
An Africa-only product — the buyers are global

03 · Tone of Voice

How We Sound

Dark editorial. Confident without bravado. The music is world-class — the infrastructure is the message.

1
Direct and practical
No fluff. No jargon for jargon's sake. If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, cut it. Every line should earn its place.
2
Specific over vague
Numbers, timelines, named territories, actual fee ranges. "11 hours brief-to-delivery" not "fast turnaround." "Showmax production" not "major streaming placement."
3
Building in public
Show the process, the numbers, the failures alongside the wins. Running totals. Vetting rejections. Honest milestone posts. Transparency is the differentiator.
4
Confident without bravado
The music is already world-class. We don't need to oversell it. State facts, let proof do the work. Never hype what can be demonstrated.
5
Never defensive about African music
African music is the asset, not the underdog. "African music belongs on global screens" — not "African music deserves recognition." Position from strength, not from gap.
DO
DON'T
Use exact numbers: "11 hours brief-to-delivery"
Say "fast turnaround" or "quick response time"
Name the placement: "South African composer, Showmax production"
Say "exciting new placement on a major streaming platform"
Show what got rejected in vetting and why
Only post wins; hide the rejection rate
"African music belongs on global screens"
"African music is underrepresented" or "deserves a chance"
Post pillar-tagged content on a planned schedule
Post reactively when something happens
Lead with the composer's story and outcome
Lead with what SyncMaster did or built
Use "sync licensing" with precision — master vs. sync, fee tiers, brief anatomy
Say "music licensing" generically or conflate sync with streaming
Let proof accumulate publicly — running totals (31 applications, 9 vetted)
Make forward-looking claims without evidence to back them
Write like a smart colleague sharing something relevant
Write like a vendor pitching a product
Use specifics about the global side: Netflix, WPP, Dentsu, Guild of Music Supervisors
Keep it vague: "global brands," "major platforms," "top agencies"
04 · Colour Palette

Colour Direction

A dark void canvas with four editorial accents. The palette should feel like a high-end editorial magazine printed in the dark.

Deep void black — the canvas everything lives on.
Warm editorial gold — the primary accent. Authority. Attention. Active states.
Electric teal — secondary accent. Education. Progress. Clarity.
Ember coral — warning and energy. Behind the Scenes. YouTube. High contrast.
Soft violet — creative depth. Instagram. Culture. Night sky.
Ghost white — primary text. Intentionally off-white to avoid harshness.
Cool grey scale — three levels of de-emphasis from mid to muted.

05 · Typography

Type Direction

Two fonts. One pairing logic: Syne commands attention, DM Sans maintains legibility and warmth at body sizes.

Display
Syne
Geometric Display Sans · Weights 700, 800
African Music.
Global Screens.
Tight letter-spacing at large sizes. Angular geometry. Used for the brand wordmark, page titles, section headers, and anywhere authority or brand presence is needed. Never use at small sizes — it breaks below 14px.
Body
DM Sans
Humanist Geometric Sans · Weights 300, 400, 500
Infrastructure for African composers. Briefs matched. Rights cleared. 48 hours.
High x-height. Warm at small sizes. Used for all body copy, UI labels, navigation, metadata, and anything under 16px. Weight 300 for long-form copy, 400 for default, 500 for emphasis labels.

Template Structures for Claude Design
Education · IG Carousel
5–7 Slide Explainer
  1. 1Hook slide — bold claim or provocative question
  2. 2Context — why this matters to Tunde specifically
  3. 3–5Core concept broken into numbered points
  4. 6Real example with specific numbers or name
  5. 7CTA — save this / share with a composer
Proof · IG Single
Placement Announcement
  1. 1Composer location + background (one line)
  2. 2Production name and type (Showmax, NGO, film)
  3. 3Fee tier range — never exact, always a bracket
  4. 4Brief-to-delivery time
  5. 5One-line on what made this composer placement-ready
BTS · IG Single
Milestone Update
  1. 1The number prominently (31 applications / 9 vetted)
  2. 2What changed this week or month
  3. 3One thing that didn't work + what we learned
  4. 4What's next (one specific next step)
Education · LinkedIn
Article Opener
  1. 1First 3 lines must stand alone (preview cutoff)
  2. 2Open with a specific observation, not a question
  3. 3Numbered breakdown (5–8 points)
  4. 4Close with proof — real number or placement name
  5. 5One CTA: "Forward this to a composer in your network"
BTS · Twitter/X
Thread Opener
  1. 1Hook tweet: specific number or bold statement alone
  2. 2–7Numbered breakdown — each tweet self-contained
  3. 8Proof tweet: placement or traction number
  4. 9Close: one ask, low friction
Culture · IG Single
African Music Statement
  1. 1Bold single sentence — no hedging
  2. 2One or two lines of context maximum
  3. 3No CTA — let the statement land on its own
  4. —Tone: proud. Declarative. Not defensive.
