# Producer Post-Demo Sequence (Amara)

## Overview
2-touch LinkedIn + email sequence triggered after a music supervisor attends a SyncMaster demo.
Picks up immediately from the warm event — does not repeat what was shown. Each touch adds
a different angle and ends with a single low-friction ask.

**Trigger condition:** Supervisor attended the 15-minute demo booked via the cold outreach sequence.
**Entry point:** Amara has seen the brief-response workflow, the 31-hour turnaround, the rights
confirmation process, and the catalogue depth. She hasn't sent a brief yet.

## Sequence Goals
- Anchor the one thing from the demo most relevant to her specific workflow
- Remove the friction point most likely to delay her first brief
- Get one real brief into the system — not a commitment, a trial

---

## Touch 1: LinkedIn Message — "The part that usually surprises people"

**Send:** Day 1 (within 24 hours of demo)
**Channel:** LinkedIn message (same thread as cold outreach if possible)
**Goal:** Anchor one concrete demo takeaway, surface and dissolve the most common post-demo
hesitation, end with one low-friction question
**Content Pillar:** Proof (specific · credible) + Behind the Scenes (honest · direct)

### Message:
```
Hi [First Name],

Good to walk you through it today.

The part most supervisors find surprising isn't the turnaround — it's the rights step.
5 hours to confirm rights on a shortlisted track sounds fast until you've watched it happen.
Every composer enters the catalogue with documentation already filed. The brief doesn't
trigger the rights work. It was done in advance.

The question I usually get at this point: "What if none of your catalogue fits my brief?"

Honest answer: sometimes it won't. We have depth in Afrofusion, cinematic, contemporary
orchestral, and organic acoustic — thinner in electronic and hip-hop. If you tell me the
brief type, I'll tell you before you send it whether we're the right call.

Do you have something coming up where African music could work?
```

### Why it works (skill alignment):
- Opens on what SHE experienced, not "great meeting you"
- The rights-work-in-advance detail is the differentiator that matters most to Amara's pain —
  it's the "so what" behind the 5-hour number
- Proactively names the honest limitation (catalogue gaps) — builds trust, removes the
  "what if it doesn't fit" objection before she has to raise it
- CTA is a question, not a request for a meeting. One word reply possible: "Yes, actually."

---

## Touch 2: Email — "A brief we turned around this week"

**Send:** Day 5 (if no reply to Touch 1)
**Channel:** Email
**Goal:** Add a new angle — a real brief story, not a repeat of the demo. Make the ask
concrete: send a brief, not a follow-up call.
**Content Pillar:** Behind the Scenes (real · operational) + Proof (specific · believable)

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "a brief from this week"
- "31 hours, start to finish"
- "what we sent back"

### Body:
```
Hi [First Name],

More context on how the process works — useful after what you saw today.

Brief came in Monday morning: 45-second open. Global NGO campaign. Hopeful, organic,
contemporary African. One-stop. Delivery window: 48 hours.

Monday 11am: 5 tracks delivered to the supervisor. Rights confirmation docs included.
Tuesday: one track shortlisted for picture lock review.
Tuesday afternoon: rights confirmation requested. 3-hour turnaround.
Wednesday: placement confirmed.

31 hours from brief to delivery. 5 hours of that was rights work. The other 26 was
creative selection — which is where the supervisor's time should go.

The brief didn't need to be African-specific. It asked for "contemporary" and "organic."
African music was the answer because it was the best fit — not because the category was
predetermined.

If you have a brief where that kind of fit is possible, I'd like to show you what we'd
send back. Reply with the brief details — even rough ones — and I'll put together a
sample response so you can see the format before committing to anything.
```

### Why it works (skill alignment):
- Completely different angle from Touch 1 — no repetition, stands alone
- Story → Bridge → Ask structure: real brief outcome, bridge ("if you have something
  similar"), low-friction ask (send rough brief details, not a commitment)
- The parenthetical "even rough ones" removes the friction of feeling like the brief
  needs to be fully formed to send
- Subject lines are 3–4 words, lowercase, read like internal project references —
  not a sales follow-up
- Ends the sequence without a breakup email — Amara is a professional contact, not a
  cold lead. If she doesn't reply to Touch 2, she moves to a quarterly re-engagement
  cadence (manual, not automated)

---

## Sequence Timing & Optimisation

### Cadence:
- Touch 1: Day 1 — same day as demo if possible, next morning at latest
- Touch 2: Day 5 — gives enough gap to feel like a separate communication, not a chase

### If she replies to Touch 1:
- Do NOT send Touch 2 on a timer — she's in a live conversation
- Respond to her reply directly; the next step is determined by what she says
- If she sends brief details: move to Brief Response workflow (outside this sequence)
- If she says "not right now": acknowledge, ask if you can reach out in 30 days

### If she replies to Touch 2:
- Same as above — live conversation takes over
- Goal shifts to: brief received → show what we'd send back → earn the real brief

### After Touch 2 with no reply:
- Do not automate further. Move to quarterly manual check-in.
- Trigger: new proof post (placement confirmed, new supervisor testimonial) → use as
  reason to re-engage: "Thought of you when this one came in — [link to proof post]"

---

## Metrics to Track

- Touch 1 reply rate (target: >35% — warm audience, not cold)
- Touch 2 reply rate (target: >20%)
- Brief received rate from sequence (target: >15% of demos attended)
- Demo-to-active-supervisor conversion rate (target: >25% within 30 days)

## Personalisation Notes

**Touch 1 personalisation levers:**
- If you know her typical brief types (from LinkedIn, her profile, the demo conversation):
  swap the catalogue gap example for a specific one relevant to her
  e.g. "We're thin on electronic — if that's a gap, I'll tell you upfront rather than
  waste your time on a brief we can't match."
- If she mentioned a specific show or campaign during the demo: open with that reference
  e.g. "The [show/campaign] brief type you mentioned is exactly the kind of thing we'd
  match well — [one sentence why]."

**Touch 2 personalisation levers:**
- If you have a different brief story more relevant to her specialty: swap the NGO
  campaign story for that one. The structure stays the same.
- If she's at an ad agency rather than a streaming platform: the "global NGO campaign"
  example resonates more than a TV placement example would. Match the brief type to her world.

---

## Implementation Notes

### Why LinkedIn first, not email:
- The cold outreach happened on LinkedIn — staying in the same channel keeps continuity
- LinkedIn messages have higher open rates post-demo than cold email
- Email is used for Touch 2 because by Day 5 a different medium signals this is a
  meaningful follow-up, not a notification

### Automation:
- Touch 1: manual send — written specifically to that supervisor within 24 hours
- Touch 2: can be templated but subject line and brief story should be adjusted per person
- No full automation recommended — this is a high-value, low-volume outreach track

### Handoff after sequence:
- Reply received → live conversation → Brief Response workflow
- No reply after Touch 2 → quarterly manual re-engagement (trigger: new proof content)
- If supervisor sends a brief that doesn't fit → honest "not our strength here" reply +
  offer to reach back when catalogue grows in that area
