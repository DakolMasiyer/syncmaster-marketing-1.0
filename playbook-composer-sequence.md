# Composer Outreach Sequence (Tunde)

## Overview
3-touch email sequence targeting African independent composers to educate, build trust, and drive applications to SyncMaster. Aligned with Education and Proof content pillars.

## Sequence Goals
- Educate on sync licensing fundamentals (Education pillar)
- Build trust through proof and transparency (Proof pillar)  
- Drive applications by lowering perceived barriers
- Align with existing content calendar themes

## Email 1: Education - "The Sync Opportunity Most African Composers Miss"

**Send**: Day 0
**Trigger**: Initial outreach (LinkedIn connection, email from scraping, event follow-up)
**Goal**: Educate, create curiosity, position SyncMaster as the guide
**Content Pillar**: Education (35% focus)

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "Nobody explained this in music school (but it's worth more)"  
- "The sync licensing gap African composers are sitting on"
- "What Netflix scenes actually pay (and how to get there)"

### Body:
```

→ Rights documented — not just owned, but provable

Most African composers are producing work that would fit global briefs. What's missing isn't the music — it's the infrastructure.

That's what sync-ready means. And that's what we built.

If you want the full breakdown of how it works, reply and I'll send it over.
```

## Email 3: Application - "Your Sync-Ready Checklist"

**Send**: Day 7 (if no reply to Email 2)  
**Trigger**: No response to second email
**Goal**: Drive action, lower barrier to apply, provide clear next steps
**Content Pillar**: Education + Proof combination

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "6 things to fix before your next supervisor pitch"  
- "The sync-ready checklist African composers actually need"
- "Are you sync-ready? Here's how to find out in 5 minutes"

### Body:
```
Hey [First Name],

Before you pitch to a single music supervisor, run this audit on your own catalogue.

Here are the 6 things every composer needs to fix first.

1/ SAMPLE CLEARANCE
Are all your samples cleared?
If you're not 100% sure → they're not cleared.
Uncleared samples disqualify a track immediately.

2/ RIGHTS DOCUMENTATION
Can you prove you own (or control) both master and publishing?
Documentation = a signed agreement or a clear paper trail.
"I own it" is not documentation.

3/ FILE FORMAT
Is every commercial track available as a WAV or AIFF?
MP3 is not broadcast spec.
If your only copy is on SoundCloud, you're not ready.

4/ ISRC CODES
Does every recording have an ISRC?
Without it, royalties can't be routed to you.
Free to get through your distributor or national music body.

5/ STEMS
Are stems available for every track in your pitch catalogue?
Not eventually. Now.
Supervisors will ask within 24 hours of shortlisting.

6/ RESPONSE SYSTEM
Can you respond to a brief in under 24 hours?
Test it before you need it.
The brief window closes whether you're ready or not.

Fix these six things. Then apply.

[P.S. We're accepting applications now. Reply with "ready" if you'd like us to review your catalogue against this list — I'll send you the link.]
```

### Alignment:
- Directly pulls from IG-M2-EDU-07 and TW-M2-SOLO-06 content
- Combines Education (teaching) + Proof (actionable checklist)
- Provides clear, low-barrier next step
- Creates urgency with "fix these six things"
