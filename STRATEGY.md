## 1. The Problem We're Solving

African composers produce world-class music. The global sync market — film, TV, advertising, games — spends billions licensing music every year. These two facts do not connect.

The gap is structural, not creative. Supervisors can't access African catalogues because rights are unclear, metadata is incomplete, stems are missing, and response times are slow. Composers can't break in because they don't have access to real briefs, don't understand what supervisors need, and have no infrastructure to compete.

SyncMaster is the infrastructure that closes that gap.

**Goal:** Turn existing catalogue into a consistent income stream from global sync  
**Pain:** Rights complexity, metadata gaps, no access to real briefs, feeling invisible despite quality

**Key messages:**
- "First placement fee could exceed 6 months of streaming income"

### Amara — The Music Supervisor
**Who:** Supervisor at a streaming platform, ad agency, or production company — global (US/UK/EU), with growing Africa interest  
**Goal:** Solve briefs on deadline with zero rights headaches and genuine discovery  
**Pain:** Hundreds of irrelevant submissions, rights clearance taking 40% of workflow, can't find authentic African music reliably

**Key messages:**
- "Solve your brief in 24 hours with rights-cleared African music"
- "Stop wasting time on clearance — we handle the paperwork"
- "Fresh discoveries that aren't in the big libraries yet"

### Nollywood Producer — Tertiary
**Who:** Nigerian film director or producer needing licensed music for theatrical or streaming  
**Goal:** Proper sync licensing without clearance complexity  
**Language:** Direct, practical, peer-to-peer

## 4. Content Strategy

### Four-Month Arc

| Phase | Month | Priority shift | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prove the model | Month 1 (June) | Education 35%, Proof 25%, BTS 20% | Build awareness, establish credibility |
| Build trust | Month 2 (July) | Education 30%, Proof 30%, BTS 20% | Deepen engagement, grow application pipeline |
| Demonstrate scale | Month 3 (Aug) | Proof 35%, BTS 25%, Education 20% | Convert — applications, brief requests, placements |
| Compound | Month 4+ | Proof 40%, Culture 20%, BTS 20% | Scale via referral and community flywheel |

Culture (15%) and Industry (5%) hold constant across all phases.

### The Five Content Pillars

**1. Education (20–35%)**  
Explain the sync infrastructure gap in plain language. Teach rights basics, metadata standards, ISRC, stems, brief formats. Position SyncMaster as the guide that makes this accessible.  
*Format: Carousels, threads, blog posts*

**2. Proof (25–40%)**  
Document every milestone publicly: applications received, composers vetted, briefs fielded, tracks pitched, placements confirmed. Specific over vague — names, numbers, timelines.  
*Format: Singles, case study posts, before/after, income stories*

**3. Behind the Scenes (20–25%)**  
Show the operational process in real time — vetting calls, brief walkthroughs, rights documentation, submission prep. Building in public as brand strategy.  
*Format: Short video, LinkedIn posts, stories*

**4. Culture (15%)**  
African music is global by default, not by exception. Position the African music ecosystem as the asset — not the underdog asking for inclusion.  
*Format: Profile posts, community moments, industry commentary*

**5. Industry (5%)**  
Signals from the sync market — supervisor trends, streaming music spend, licensing shifts. Positions SyncMaster as an informed insider, not just an aggregator.  
*Format: LinkedIn threads, commentary posts*

## 6. Outreach System

Two parallel outreach tracks operate simultaneously.

### Composer Track (Tunde)

| Sequence | Trigger | Touches | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold outreach | Identified via Facebook groups, WhatsApp, events | 3 emails over 7 days | Email / LinkedIn | Application submitted |
| Onboarding | Application accepted | 4 emails over 10 days | Email | Composer goes live (metadata clean, stems ready, rights confirmed) |
| Monthly newsletter | No reply after cold sequence Email 3 | 1x/month | Email | Re-engage, keep warm, capture when timing is right |

**Cold sequence arc:** Day 0 intro (who we are, what we do, why now) → Day 3 education (sync explained, metadata hook) → Day 7 social proof (placement story + application CTA)

**Onboarding arc:** Day 0 acceptance (welcome + next steps) → Day 2 metadata walkthrough → Day 5 stems + rights docs → Day 10 "you're live" activation

### Producer Track (Amara)

| Sequence | Trigger | Touches | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold outreach | LinkedIn connection accepted | 3 messages over 7 days | LinkedIn | Demo booked |
| Post-demo | Demo attended | 2 touches over 5 days | LinkedIn + Email | First brief submitted |

**Cold sequence arc:** Day 0 value open (reference their brief history, lead with relevance) → Day 3 proof (rights-clearance time saved, response stats) → Day 7 low-friction CTA (brief trial offer)

**Post-demo arc:** Day 1 recap + one takeaway → Day 5 first brief invitation

## 7. Brand Voice

- **Direct and practical.** No fluff. No "transforming the industry" abstraction.
- **Specific over vague.** Numbers, timelines, territories, actual fees. "31 hours brief-to-delivery" beats "fast turnaround."
- **Building in public.** Show the process, the numbers, even the failures. Transparency is a differentiator.
- **Confident without bravado.** The music is world-class. The infrastructure is the message. African music doesn't need to ask for a seat.
- **Never defensive about African music.** Position it as the asset, not the underdog.

## 11. Objection Handling Summary

### Composer objections
1. "Rights are too complicated" → Guide framing + proof (9 composers cleared in Month 2)
2. "No time for metadata" → Investment reframe (3x more brief matches)
3. "Why trust you with my music?" → Transparency + 100% masters retained
4. "Is there money for African composers?" → Specific placement fees, named productions
5. "I tried before and got nowhere" → Structured approach vs cold pitching (31h delivery)

### Supervisor objections
1. "No time to vet new sources" → Time-saver positioning (5h vs 9h on clearance)
2. "How clean are the rights?" → Zero issues in Month 2 placements, documentation provided upfront
3. "What if the composer disappears?" → <24h response time guarantee
4. "Is the quality actually there?" → Range proof (orchestral, organic, cinematic)
5. "I already have go-to sources" → Complementary positioning (fills the African catalogue gap)
