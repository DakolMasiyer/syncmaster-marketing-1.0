## 1. PRODUCT & POSITIONING

**SyncMaster** is a sync licensing infrastructure platform.  
It bridges African composers and global music supervisors (film, TV, ads, games).

**Core value proposition:**
- To composers: get your music into global productions — legally clean, professionally submitted, brief-matched — without a label or agent.
- To music supervisors: receive 5 curated, rights-cleared, brief-matched African tracks in 48 hours. No clearance admin.

**Category framing:** Not a music library. Not a label. Infrastructure that makes African composers commercially viable in the global sync market.

**Against:** General music libraries (don't vet for sync-readiness), labels (don't service independent composers), cold-pitch culture (SyncMaster replaces it with brief-matched structured responses).

**Differentiation:**
- Speed: 24–48h turnaround from brief receipt to delivery
- Vetting: every composer is rights-confirmed, metadata-cleaned, stems-prepared before a brief arrives
- One-stop cleared: one conversation, one deal — no split ownership complications
- Focus: exclusively African composers, global market ambition
- Building in public: the whole process is documented and posted in real time

**What makes it different from cold pitching:**  
Composers don't cold-submit. Briefs arrive, SyncMaster matches, composers respond within 24h to pre-vetted opportunities.

## 2. PERSONAS

### Tunde (Primary — Composer)
- Independent African composer (Lagos, Abuja, Nairobi, Cape Town, Accra)
- 2–15 years composing for local projects
- Has professional-quality work but has never earned from sync
- Understands streaming; doesn't understand sync infrastructure
- **Pains:** no access to real briefs, rights confusion, metadata chaos (no ISRC, bad tagging), missing stems, feels invisible despite quality work
- **Goals:** first placement fee, sustainable sync income, global recognition, IMDb credits
- **Objections:** "rights are too complicated," "I don't have time for metadata," "Is there really money in this for African composers?"
- **Messaging that resonates:** "Your music is ready — let's get it paid for," "First placement fee could be more than 6 months of streaming," "We handle the complexity so you can focus on creating"
- **Where to find:** Facebook composer groups, WhatsApp networks, Instagram music communities, LinkedIn, VI-Control, Scorecast, Discord music servers

### Amara (Secondary — Music Supervisor / Production Company)
- Music supervisor at streaming platform, ad agency, or film production
- Location: Global (US, UK, EU with growing Africa interest)
- 5+ years supervision/production experience
- Budget authority: mid-level (can approve <$50K music budgets)
- **Pains:** hundreds of irrelevant submissions, rights clearance taking 40% of workflow time, can't find authentic African music easily, submissions missing stems/metadata/ISRC, unclear ownership
- **Goals:** solve briefs under deadline, reduce clearance headaches, discover fresh authentic sounds
- **Objections:** "I don't have time to vet new sources," "How do I know rights are clean?", "I already have go-to sources"
- **Messaging that resonates:** "Solve your brief in 24 hours with rights-cleared African music," "Zero rights issues — documentation provided upfront," "Fresh discoveries not in the big libraries yet"
- **Where to find:** LinkedIn, Guild of Music Supervisors, Sync Summit, MIDEM, Music Week, Twitter/X, production companies, ad agencies, streaming platforms

### Nollywood (Tertiary — Nigerian Film Production)
- Nigerian film director/producer needing original music for theatrical or streaming release
- Uses music informally; wants to graduate to proper sync licensing
- Pain: finding one-stop cleared music without clearance drama
- Tone: direct, practical, peer-to-peer

## 7. OBJECTION HANDLING

### Composer Objections

**"I don't understand rights — it's too complicated"**
- Acknowledge: rights terminology is confusing
- Reframe: SyncMaster is the guide that simplifies it
- Proof: "We've helped 9 composers clear rights in Month 2 — none had prior sync experience"

**"I don't have time to fix my metadata/stems"**
- Reframe: metadata isn't admin — it's what makes your music discoverable
- Proof: "Composers with complete metadata get 3x more brief matches"

**"Why should I trust you with my music?"**
- Emphasize: composer retains 100% of rights and masters
- Proof: "All 9 vetted composers in Month 2 retained 100% of rights"

**"Is there really money in this for African composers?"**
- Proof: "Lagos composer earned more from one Netflix scene than 6 months of streaming"
- Proof: First placement fee potential: $5,000–$20,000 for one scene

**"I tried before and got nowhere"**
- Differentiate: structured brief-matching vs. random submissions
- Proof: "Average brief-to-delivery 31 hours in Month 2"

### Producer Objections

**"I don't have time to vet new sources"**
- Reframe: time-saver, not addition — reduces clearance workload
- Proof: "Supervisors using SyncMaster spend 5 hours on rights confirmation (was 9)"

**"How do I know rights are clean?"**
- Show: documentation process and verification steps
- Proof: "0 rights issues in Month 2 — all documentation provided upfront"

**"I already have go-to sources — why switch?"**
- Complementary value: fills gaps in existing sources (authentic African music no one else has)
- Proof: "We've matched briefs for orchestral Afrofusion, hopeful organic, and cinematic tense"
