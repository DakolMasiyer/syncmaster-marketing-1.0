# Composer Onboarding Sequence (Tunde)

## Overview
4-touch email sequence triggered when a composer's application is accepted. Takes them from acceptance to a live, brief-ready catalogue in 10 days. Aligned with Education and Behind the Scenes content pillars.

**Trigger condition:** Application reviewed and accepted by SyncMaster team.  
**Entry point:** Composer has already received the cold outreach sequence (knows sync basics, has done the 6-point self-audit). Onboarding picks up from there — execution, not education.

## Sequence Goals
- Confirm acceptance and set clear expectations for the onboarding window
- Get metadata, ISRC codes, and file formats submission-ready
- Confirm stems are packaged and rights documentation is filed
- Activate the composer in the catalogue and brief-matching system
- Build confidence: first brief could arrive within weeks

## Email 1: Welcome — "You're In"

**Trigger**: Application accepted by SyncMaster team
**Goal**: Welcome, set 10-day onboarding timeline, get first submission started
**Content Pillar**: Behind the Scenes (personal · honest · direct)

- "You're in — here's what happens next"

- "SyncMaster: your catalogue goes live in 10 days"

### Body:
```
Hey [First Name],

Your application has been reviewed. You're in.

Here's what the next 10 days look like:

Day 0–2:   Metadata and ISRC check
Day 2–5:   File format and stems confirmation
Day 5–10:  Rights documentation and catalogue activation
Day 10+:   You're live — briefs start matching to your catalogue

Before we go further — a straight answer to the question most composers ask first:

"How long until I get a brief?"

On average, composers in our system receive their first brief match within 3–5 weeks of going live. Some faster. It depends on catalogue depth, genre fit with active briefs, and how quickly we get through onboarding.

The onboarding window is the part you control. This 10-day window is yours to move through as fast as you want.

First step — reply to this email with 3 tracks you want in your initial pitch catalogue. One should represent your strongest commercial work. One should show range. One can be whatever you think supervisors haven't heard from African composers yet.

We'll start the metadata review from there.

Welcome to SyncMaster.

[Your name]
SyncMaster
```

## Email 2: Metadata & ISRC — "The Foundation"

**Send**: Day 2 (after first tracks submitted, or 48 hours after Email 1)
**Trigger**: Composer replies with tracks, or 48-hour timer if no reply
**Goal**: Walk through metadata requirements, confirm ISRC registration, fix gaps
**Content Pillar**: Education (informative · accessible · step-by-step)

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "Metadata check — here's what we found and what to fix"
- "3 metadata issues that block placements (and how to fix them this week)"
- "Your catalogue review: what's ready and what isn't"

### Body:
```
Hey [First Name],

We've looked at your tracks. Here's the honest assessment.

Metadata is the part most composers skip. It's also the part that determines whether a supervisor ever finds your music — and whether royalties reach you when they do.

Here are the 4 fields that matter most for sync placements:

1/ TITLE & COMPOSER CREDITS
Exactly as they appear on your publishing registration.
Even one character difference creates a rights dispute.
Check it now against your PRO registration.

2/ ISRC CODES
Every commercial recording needs one.
If you don't have ISRCs yet: your distributor issues them for free.
DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby — all do this automatically on upload.
If you're self-distributing, your national music body can assign them.

3/ BPM, KEY, MOOD TAGS
Supervisors search by feel before they search by genre.
"Hopeful, organic, 90 BPM" gets a brief match.
"Afrobeats" alone does not.
Add at least 3 mood tags and 2 instrumentation tags per track.

4/ DURATION
Every track needs a full-length version and a 30-second edit.
Most briefs specify duration. If you only have one version, you lose half the briefs automatically.

Reply with your current metadata spreadsheet (or we'll send you our template).
We'll flag any gaps and help you fix them before the file submission step.

The ISRC and credits check is the one thing that can delay your go-live — so let's get it confirmed this week.

[Your name]
SyncMaster
```

### Alignment:
- Education pillar tone: informative, step-by-step, no jargon
- Directly addresses "I don't have time to fix my metadata" objection with actionable steps
- Builds on what Tunde already learned in cold outreach Email 3 (the 6-point checklist)
- Low-barrier CTA: just send a spreadsheet or use the template

## Email 3: Stems & Rights — "The Two Things Supervisors Check First"

**Send**: Day 5 (after metadata confirmed, or 72 hours after Email 2)
**Trigger**: Metadata confirmed clean, or timer
**Goal**: Confirm stems are packaged correctly, rights documentation filed and verified
**Content Pillar**: Education + Behind the Scenes

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "Stems and rights — the two things every supervisor checks before shortlisting"
- "Your stems review: what we need before you go live"
- "One step from live — stems and rights confirmation"

### Body:
```
Hey [First Name],

Two things left before you're live.

Both are things supervisors check in the first 10 minutes of reviewing a submission. Both can end a placement before it starts.

─────────────────────────
STEMS
─────────────────────────

What we need per track:
→ Full mix (WAV, 24-bit, 44.1kHz minimum)
→ Instrumental (no vocals)
→ Stems: at minimum — drums, bass, melodic elements, full without drums

Why it matters:
A brief comes in on Monday. We shortlist your track Tuesday. Supervisor wants stems by Wednesday for picture lock Thursday. If you don't have stems ready, your track is replaced — regardless of how good it is.

We've lost two placements to this exact scenario.

If your stems aren't packaged yet: this week is the time. Start with your 3 pitch tracks from Email 1. We only need stems for the catalogue tracks — you don't need to stem your entire back catalogue.

─────────────────────────
RIGHTS DOCUMENTATION
─────────────────────────

What we need confirmed:
→ You own (or fully control) both master recording and publishing rights
→ No uncleared samples anywhere in the pitch catalogue
→ If you co-wrote with anyone: written agreement assigning sync licensing rights to you, or confirming you can licence on their behalf

What this looks like in practice:
→ Self-released: a signed split sheet with all co-writers (even if the split is 100/0)
→ Label-signed: a clause in your contract confirming sync licensing rights
→ "I own it" is not documentation — a supervisor's legal team needs a paper trail

If anything is unclear: flag it now. We'd rather delay your go-live by a week than have a rights issue surface mid-negotiation. Supervisors who hit a rights problem with a source don't come back.

Reply with your stems upload link and your rights confirmation.
We'll do a final check and confirm your go-live date.

[Your name]
SyncMaster
```

### Alignment:
- Behind the Scenes tone on the stems section: real story, personal, direct
- Education tone on rights: step-by-step, non-threatening
- Addresses "Why should I trust you with my music?" objection — shows SyncMaster is protecting composer rights, not taking them
- The "we've lost two placements to this exact scenario" is a credibility-building detail from Proof pillar

## Email 4: Activation — "You're Live"

**Send**: Day 10 (after stems and rights confirmed)
**Trigger**: All onboarding steps cleared — composer is activated in catalogue
**Goal**: Confirm go-live, explain brief-matching process, set expectations for first brief, hand off to ongoing relationship
**Content Pillar**: Proof + Behind the Scenes

### Subject Lines (A/B Test):
- "You're live — here's how brief matching works"
- "Catalogue active. First match could come this week."
- "SyncMaster: you're in the brief pool"

### Body:
```
Hey [First Name],

You're live.

Your catalogue is active in our brief-matching system as of today.

Here's exactly what happens when a brief comes in:

Monday 9am
Brief arrives. "45-second open. Global campaign. Hopeful, organic, contemporary African. One-stop. Delivery: 48 hours."

Monday 9–11am
We search the catalogue. Your tracks — and everyone else's — are reviewed against the brief. We shortlist 5 options maximum. One per composer where possible.

Monday 11am–2pm
You receive a WhatsApp message: "Brief available. Can you confirm track [X] is still available for one-stop licensing?" Response window: 4 hours.

This is the moment where composers either stay in the brief or fall out. A 4-hour response window is not a suggestion. Supervisors move to the next option without explanation.

Tuesday
If you're shortlisted: rights confirmation requested. Stems if not already on file. Our target: 3-hour turnaround on rights confirmation.

Wednesday
Placement confirmed or track not selected. Either way, you get a full debrief.

─────────────────────────

What to expect in the next 30 days:
→ First brief match: typically 3–5 weeks from go-live
→ First shortlisting: depends on catalogue fit and brief volume
→ First placement: no timeline we can promise — but every composer in Month 2 who went through this exact onboarding received at least one brief match within 6 weeks

Keep your stems folder ready. Keep your phone notifications on. Keep your rights documentation updated if anything changes.

One last thing: if your catalogue grows, send us new tracks. The wider your catalogue, the more briefs you match. You're not limited to the 3 tracks from onboarding.

Good to have you in.

[Your name]
SyncMaster
```

### Alignment:
- Pulls directly from IG-M2-BTS-02 (brief response week breakdown) and producer sequence Message 3
- Proof pillar: "every composer in Month 2 who went through this onboarding received at least one brief match within 6 weeks"
- Behind the Scenes: real timeline, real WhatsApp message example, real consequences of missing the window
- Ends with relationship continuation — not a hard close, just "you're in"

## Sequence Timing & Optimization

### Cadence:
- Email 1: Day 0 (acceptance day)
- Email 2: Day 2 (or on track submission — whichever comes first)
- Email 3: Day 5 (or on metadata confirmation — whichever comes first)
- Email 4: Day 10 (on full onboarding sign-off — not before)

### Accelerated path:
A motivated composer can complete this in 4–5 days if they have metadata, ISRCs, stems, and rights docs ready. Email 4 should only go when the go-live is confirmed — do not send on a timer if onboarding is incomplete.

### A/B Testing Priorities:
1. Email 1 subject line (sets tone for entire sequence)
2. Email 3 stems explanation (most common sticking point)
3. Email 4 brief-matching detail level (does more specificity increase confidence or anxiety?)

### Metrics to Track:
- Email 1 → Email 2 completion rate (target: >80%)
- Average days to go-live (target: <12 days)
- Stem submission rate by Day 7 (target: >70%)
- Rights issues flagged during onboarding (track to reduce over time)
- Composer response time to first brief match (target: <2 hours)
