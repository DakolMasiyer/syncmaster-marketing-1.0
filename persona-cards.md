# SyncMaster Persona Cards

## Composer Persona: Tunde

### Demographics
- **Role**: Independent Film/TV Composer, Indie Artist, Music Producer
- **Location**: Primarily Africa (Nigeria, South Africa, Kenya, Ghana focus)
- **Experience**: 2-15 years composing for local projects
- **Education**: Self-taught or formal music education
- **Income**: Variable, supplementing with other music work (teaching, performing)

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal**: Get music placed in global film, TV, ads, and games
- **Secondary Goals**: 
  - Earn sustainable income from music
  - Gain international recognition
  - Break into the global sync market
  - Validate talent on world stage
- **Success Metrics**: 
  - First placement fee earned
  - Repeat placements
  - Royalty income stream
  - IMDb/screen credits

### Daily Pains & Frustrations
- No access to real sync briefs
- Don't know what music supervisors actually want
- Rights confusion (samples, ownership, publishing)
- Metadata chaos (no ISRC, bad tagging)
- Slow/no responses to submissions
- Feel invisible despite quality work
- Missing stems when requested
- Don't understand sync licensing fundamentals

### Top Objections You'll Hear
1. "I don't understand rights - it's too complicated"
2. "I don't have time to fix my metadata/stems"
3. "Why should I trust you with my music?"
4. "Is there really money in this for African composers?"
5. "I tried before and got nowhere"

### What Success Looks Like for Tunde
- Has ISRC codes on all commercial tracks
- Has stems ready for every pitch track
- Knows exactly what metadata supervisors need
- Understands sync licensing basics
- Can respond to briefs in <24 hours
- Has clear rights documentation
- Gets first placement and payment
- Starts earning performance royalties

### Where to Find Tunde
- Facebook composer groups
- WhatsApp composer networks
- Instagram music communities
- LinkedIn (seeking opportunities)
- Local music schools/universities
- VI-Control, Scorecast communities
- Discord music production servers
- local gigs and performances

### Messaging That Resonates
- "Your music is ready - let's get it paid for"
- "We handle the complexity so you can focus on creating"
- "First placement fee could be more than 6 months of streaming"
- "We vet for quality so supervisors trust your submissions"
- "Infrastructure that finally connects African talent to global briefs"

### Evaluation Criteria
When Tunde considers SyncMaster, he asks:
- Is this legitimate? (proof of placements)
- Will they actually get me paid?
- How much work is this for me?
- What do I need to prepare?
- How long until I see results?
- What if I'm not ready?

## Producer Persona: Amara

### Demographics
- **Role**: Music Supervisor, Creative Producer, Ads Agency Producer, Streaming Exec
- **Location**: Global (US, UK, EU focus with growing Africa interest)
- **Experience**: 5+ years in supervision/production
- **Education**: Film, music, or media production background
- **Budget Authority**: Mid-level (can approve <$50K music budgets)

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal**: Find the perfect music for briefs quickly and legally
- **Secondary Goals**: 
  - Solve briefs under deadline pressure
  - Reduce rights clearance headaches
  - Discover fresh, authentic sounds
  - Build reputation for great music choices
- **Success Metrics**: 
  - Briefs solved on time
  - Zero rights issues post-placement
  - Director/producer happiness with music
  - Fresh discoveries that elevate projects
  - Efficient workflow (less admin, more creativity)

### Daily Pains & Frustrations
- Hundreds of irrelevant submissions per brief
- Rights clearance taking 40% of workflow time
- Can't find authentic African music easily
- Submissions missing stems, metadata, ISRC
- Composers disappearing after shortlisting
- Unclear rights ownership (samples, splits)
- Slow composer response times (days, not hours)
- Quality varies wildly in submissions

### Top Objections You'll Hear
1. "I don't have time to vet new sources"
2. "How do I know the rights are clean?"
3. "What if the composer disappears after I pick a track?"
4. "Is the quality actually there for my brief?"
5. "I already have go-to sources - why switch?"

### What Success Looks Like for Amara
- Receives 3-5 perfectly matched options per brief
- All tracks have clear, documented rights
- Stems and metadata included with every submission
- Composer responds within 24 hours
- Zero rights issues during clearance
- Discovers fresh African talent that elevates projects
- Becomes known for finding great authentic music

### Where to Find Amara
- LinkedIn (music supervisor groups)
- Guild of Music Supervisors
- Twitter/X (following directors, producers)
- Industry events (Sync Summit, MIDEM, Music Week)
- Production companies, ad agencies
- Streaming platform music departments
- Referrals from other supervisors

### Messaging That Resonates
- "Solve your brief in 24 hours with rights-cleared African music"
- "Stop wasting time on clearance - we handle the paperwork"
- "Get authentic African stems, metadata, and ISRC-ready tracks"
- "One-stop licensing from composers who own their masters"
- "Fresh discoveries that aren't in the big libraries yet"

### Evaluation Criteria
When Amara considers SyncMaster, she asks:
- How fast can I get options?
- Are the rights actually clear?
- Do they understand my brief?
- What's the quality/relevance like?
- What's the workload for me?
- Can I trust the composer to deliver?
