# Why global music supervisors are missing Africa's best composers

## Hook

The infrastructure gap between African composers and global sync supervisors is not a talent problem.

## Body

Month 1 of building SyncMaster has confirmed everything I suspected — and added a few things I didn't expect.

Here's what I've observed:

Every music supervisor I've spoken to this month has expressed genuine interest in authentic African music for their projects. Not "African-influenced." African. The demand is not hypothetical. It's explicit.

Every composer we've vetted is producing work that would compete at any level in any global sync library.

The problem is entirely in the middle:

Rights documentation that doesn't meet international standards. Metadata that doesn't exist in any searchable form. Files in the wrong format. No established relationship between two groups of professionals who need each other.

The talent gap doesn't exist.
The infrastructure gap is enormous.

What we're building at SyncMaster:
→ A vetting process that prepares African composers for international sync standards
→ A catalogue system that makes African music findable and clearable
→ Direct relationships with supervisors who are actively looking for this music
→ A brief-response process that competes with established sync libraries on speed

Month 1: 6 vetted composers. 3 briefs. 1 confirmed placement.

The market exists. The music exists. The bridge is being built.
