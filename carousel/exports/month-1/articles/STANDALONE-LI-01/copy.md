# What Month 1 taught us about the sync gap

## Hook

One month ago we launched SyncMaster.

## Body

Here are five things Month 1 taught us about the infrastructure gap between African composers and global sync.

1. The rights problem is bigger than we expected.
Of 20 applications, 9 had rights issues complex enough to disqualify them. Labels holding masters. Uncleared samples in otherwise commercial-quality work. Publishing registered incorrectly.

2. Supervisors are actively looking.
Every supervisor we've spoken to this month has expressed genuine, specific interest in African music for their projects. The demand is not hypothetical.

3. Speed is the most underestimated variable.
The two briefs we didn't win this month were both lost on response time. Not on music quality. Not on rights. On time.

4. The vetting process is the product.
Composers who pass our vetting don't just get into a library — they get a thorough audit of their rights, metadata, and files. Many said it was the first time anyone had explained any of this clearly.

5. Building in public accelerates everything.
Application volume doubled in Week 3, when we started posting transparently about what the vetting process actually involves.

The gap is real. The market is ready. The bridge is being built.
