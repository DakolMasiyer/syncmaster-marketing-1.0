# The global sync market and Africa\

## Hook

Most African composers I've spoken to recently have never had sync explained to them clearly.

## Body

Not because they're not serious about their craft. Because nobody in their network has been in the room where it happens.

Here's what I wish someone had said earlier:

Sync licensing is when your music is licensed for use in visual media — films, TV, ads, games. The composer grants permission. Gets paid upfront. Keeps the rights.

One 30-second placement in a major TV spot can pay more than a year of streaming royalties.

The global sync market generates over $500M annually.
African composers represent a fraction of a percent of that revenue.

This is not a talent gap. The music exists. The talent is extraordinary.

It's an infrastructure gap — the connections, the catalogue preparation, the rights management, the pitch system. None of it exists at scale for African composers.

That's the problem SyncMaster is solving. One vetted composer at a time.
