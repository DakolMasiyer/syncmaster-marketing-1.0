### TWEET 2/7

2/ Sync licensing = your music placed in a film, TV show, ad, or game.
The composer gets paid upfront. Not per stream. Not per play.

A 30-second placement in a premium ad: $10,000–$75,000.
One Netflix scene: $5,000–$20,000.

---
### TWEET 3/7

3/ The global sync market generates over $500M annually.

African music is in global productions constantly — Afrobeats in every luxury ad, every film with a Lagos scene, every curated "diverse" campaign.

The composers being paid for it? Mostly not African.

---
### TWEET 4/7

4/ Why? It's not the music.

It's the infrastructure:
→ No one to pitch the tracks
→ Rights not properly cleared
→ No metadata
→ No relationship with supervisors
→ No one who knows the process

---
### TWEET 5/7  ⚠ 282 chars (over 280 limit)

5/ What sync-ready actually means:
→ You own or control your master + publishing
→ No uncleared samples
→ High-quality WAV files available
→ ISRC codes assigned
→ Stems ready on request
→ Sub-24-hour response time

Most African composers fail on 3–6. None of those are about talent.

---
### TWEET 6/7

6/ The music supervisor's job is not to discover you.

They're solving a brief. Mood, tempo, duration, instrumentation, one-stop status.

They need your catalogue findable, searchable, and legally clean. Not your SoundCloud link.
