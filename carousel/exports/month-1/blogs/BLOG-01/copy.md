# Sync Licensing 101: The African Composer\

## Hook

Sync Licensing 101: The African Composer's Guide to Getting Paid for Film, TV & Ads

## Body

If you've been making music for any length of time and nobody has explained sync licensing to you clearly yet — this is that guide.

Sync licensing is the process by which your music is legally placed in visual media: film, television, advertising, games, and digital content. Every time you hear a track underscore a Netflix scene, power a global ad campaign, or set the tone in a documentary, a sync licence was negotiated.

And the composer got paid. Not in fractions of a cent. Upfront.

One 30-second placement in a premium TV ad can pay $10,000–$75,000.
A Netflix scene: $5,000–$20,000.
An indie film: $5,000–$15,000+.

The global sync market generates over $500M annually. African composers represent a fraction of a percent of that revenue.

This article covers: what sync licensing is, who the players are, what placements pay, what rights you need, and how to position your catalogue for this market.
