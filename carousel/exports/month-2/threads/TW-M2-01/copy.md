### TWEET 1/8

1/ You got a sync placement offer.

---
### TWEET 2/8

2/ The upfront sync fee is rarely the ceiling.

Especially for branded content: the first offer is an opening position, not a fixed price.

Know the market rate before you respond.

---
### TWEET 3/8

3/ Exclusivity period.

"Exclusive" means they lock your track for a window.
Six months: reasonable.
Two years: should cost significantly more.

The longer the exclusivity, the higher the fee.

---
### TWEET 4/8

4/ Territory matters more than most composers realise.

UK-only vs. global use = fundamentally different deals.

A global campaign is worth 3–5× a regional one.
Define the territory before you agree to the rate.

---
### TWEET 5/8

5/ Term length is the most overlooked variable.

One-off vs. rolling vs. in-perpetuity are three different contracts.

In-perpetuity means permanent use. Price it accordingly.

---
### TWEET 6/8

6/ Performance royalties are separate from your sync fee.

Every time the content airs, royalties generate through your PRO.

SAMRO. COSON. SOCAN. PRS. ASCAP.

Not registered? That income goes uncollected. Permanently.

---
### TWEET 7/8

7/ What's not negotiable:
→ Delivery format (broadcast-spec WAV)
→ Payment timeline (net 30 or 60)
→ Credit attribution

Focus your energy on the five things that move the number.
