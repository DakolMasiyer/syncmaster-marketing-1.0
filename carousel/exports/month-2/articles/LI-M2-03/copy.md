# Why African music is becoming the default sound of global campaigns

## Hook

Three years ago, African music in a global campaign was a creative choice.

## Body

Today, it's increasingly the expected one.

Here's what I'm seeing from inside SyncMaster after two months of handling briefs:

BRIEF LANGUAGE HAS CHANGED
The briefs we receive no longer say "include African influence." They specify "Afrofusion, Lagos sound, contemporary Afro-soul." The vocabulary has become precise. Supervisors who know what they're asking for have studied the genre deeply.

THE DEMAND IS UPSTREAM OF MUSIC SUPERVISION
This isn't supervisors developing taste independently. Global brands are asking for African soundtracks because their clients want them — for campaigns targeting African markets, and increasingly for global campaigns where African cultural currency is the creative asset.

THE DIVERSITY NARRATIVE IS BECOMING REDUNDANT
We're past the point where African music gets placed as representation. The compositions are commercially competitive, structurally ready for visual media, and genuinely the right sound for the content being made. That's the conversation now.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR COMPOSERS
The market opportunity is real and it's now. The composers who've done the infrastructure work are positioned to capture it. The ones who haven't are watching the opportunity pass.

The talent was always here. The moment has arrived.
