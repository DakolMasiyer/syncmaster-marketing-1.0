# What Month 2 is teaching us about the sync market

## Hook

Month 2 of SyncMaster is teaching us something we didn't fully expect.

## Body

The supervisors aren't just open to African music. They're actively searching for it.

In Month 2, we've had three new brief requests from supervisors in markets we hadn't directly approached — a European documentary production, a North American streaming platform, a global NGO campaign.

None of these came from outbound pitching. They came from content — from building in public and documenting what we're actually doing.

But here's what the data behind those briefs shows:

THE DEMAND IS SPECIFIC, NOT GENERIC
The briefs don't ask for "world music." They specify instrumentation, mood vocabulary, and cultural reference points with precision. Supervisors who know what they want from African music can now find someone to deliver it cleanly.

THE BOTTLENECK IS STILL INFRASTRUCTURE
Of 31 applications, 9 are in the active catalogue. The other 22 failed on rights clarity, metadata, or response speed — not music quality. The infrastructure gap is closing, but slowly.

THE COMPOUNDING EFFECT IS REAL
Every placement builds supervisor trust. Every trusted supervisor sends a new brief. The flywheel is starting.

Month 3 starts with a deeper catalogue and faster brief-response infrastructure. The pipeline is building.
