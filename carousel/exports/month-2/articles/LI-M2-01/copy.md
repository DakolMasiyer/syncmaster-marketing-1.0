# What composers get wrong about sync deal terms

## Hook

Most African composers don't know what's negotiable in a sync deal.

## Body

I've had this exact conversation more times than I can count — so here it is in writing.

When a placement offer arrives, these five things are open for discussion:

UPFRONT FEE
The first offer is a negotiation opening, not a fixed price. For branded content especially, the budget almost always has room. Ask once, clearly. Most supervisors expect it.

EXCLUSIVITY PERIOD
Six months is reasonable. Two years or more should cost significantly more than a standard placement fee. Know the difference before you agree.

TERRITORY
Global use and regional use are different deals entirely. A track used globally is worth 3–5× a regional placement. If the territory isn't defined in the offer — define it before you respond.

TERM LENGTH
One-off, rolling, and in-perpetuity licences each carry a different price point. In-perpetuity means permanent use. That comes at a premium.

PERFORMANCE ROYALTIES
Separate from the upfront sync fee and often larger over time. Every airing generates royalties through your PRO. Register before any placement is signed — there is no retroactive collection.

What's typically not negotiable: delivery spec (broadcast-spec WAV), payment timeline (net 30 or 60), and credit attribution.

The goal is to be professional and informed — not adversarial. Most supervisors respect a composer who knows their worth.
