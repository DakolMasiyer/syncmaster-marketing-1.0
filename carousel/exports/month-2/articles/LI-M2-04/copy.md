# Building SyncMaster Month 2: what's changed

## Hook

One month into Month 2. Here's the honest operational update.

## Body

WHAT'S IMPROVED:
Brief-response time is down. Month 1 average: 31 hours. Month 2 average: 22 hours. The infrastructure is faster because the catalogue is better organised — every track tagged, every stem cloud-accessible, every rights document pre-confirmed.

Repeat business has started. Two supervisors from Month 1 returned with new briefs. That's the signal that the service level is working.

WHAT WE'RE STILL FIXING:
Simultaneous brief management. Two briefs in one week works. Three concurrent would create friction. Building that capacity now.

Composer onboarding time. Average vetting duration is 12 days. Target is 7. The delay is almost always at rights documentation — composers who haven't confirmed master ownership or PRO registration in writing.

WHAT I DIDN'T EXPECT:
The inbound has started. A supervisor reached out this week without any outreach from our side. Building in public — posting the numbers, the process, the briefs — is building inbound credibility.

THE MONTH 3 FOCUS:
Scale the catalogue. Build simultaneous brief infrastructure. Start composer spotlights. Expand into UK production market.

The model is working. Now it needs to work at higher volume.
