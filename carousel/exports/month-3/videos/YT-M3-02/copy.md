# How to build a catalogue for international sync markets

## Hook

[MONTH 3 VIDEO — How to Build a Catalogue for International Sync Markets]

## Body

INTRO (0:00–0:45):
"After 90 days and 9 briefs, we know exactly what international sync markets want. This video is the practical guide to building the catalogue they're looking for."

SECTION 1 — THE MARKET AUDIT (0:45–3:00):
Start with where you want to place music: UK, US, EU, streaming, brand, or film. Each territory has different requirements. Build a market matrix: 5 columns (territory/category), rate your catalogue against each one.

SECTION 2 — THE METADATA FOUNDATION (3:00–6:00):
Without complete metadata, your tracks cannot be submitted. Walk through the complete metadata card we use for every track: ISRC, title, composer, publisher, BPM, key, mood, instrumentation, placement type, stems status, rights status. Every field explained.

SECTION 3 — THE STEMS REQUIREMENT (6:00–8:30):
International briefs always request stems. What a stem pack includes: full mix, instrumental, vocals only, stems by instrument group. File naming conventions. Why naming matters: a badly named stems folder gets rejected before the music is heard.

SECTION 4 — THE ONE-STOP REQUIREMENT (8:30–10:30):
UK supervisors will not proceed without one-stop confirmation. What one-stop means for different composer situations: self-published (easiest path), label-distributed (check your contract for sync approval rights), co-written (all co-writers must confirm before any brief is submitted).

SECTION 5 — THE 90-DAY BUILD PLAN (10:30–13:00):
Month 1: Complete metadata on 20 tracks.
Month 2: Add stems for all 20. Register PRO. Confirm one-stop.
Month 3: Submit through SyncMaster or directly. Handle brief response.

OUTRO (13:00–13:30):
"The music is already there. This is the infrastructure that makes it submittable. Apply at syncmaster.io."
